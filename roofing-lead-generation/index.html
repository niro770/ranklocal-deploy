Roofing Lead Generation Services: What's Inside and What It Costs

Every roofer's heard the pitch. "We'll flood you with leads." Then the leads show up, shared, slow, half of them tire-kickers, and "lead generation" becomes a swear word around the office. The problem usually isn't the idea. It's that "roofing lead generation service" describes ten different things sold under one name, and most roofers buy without knowing which one they signed up for.

So let's pull it apart. What these services actually do, the channels behind them, what's fair to pay, and how to tell a real program from a lead reseller with a logo.

Roofing lead generation is the work of attracting homeowners who need roof work and turning them into contacts, calls, or booked jobs for your company. A good service owns the whole chain, traffic, capture, qualification, not just the part where they email you a phone number.

What a roofing lead generation service includes

The good ones bundle four things. The weak ones sell you one and call it the whole package.

Traffic generation. Somebody has to create the demand, Google Search Ads, Local Services Ads, SEO, paid social, directories. This is the engine. If a "lead gen service" doesn't control any traffic and just resells aggregator form fills, you're buying a middleman.

Lead capture and tracking. Landing pages, call tracking numbers, recorded calls, a dashboard. Without tracking you can't tell which leads are real, which closed, or what you actually paid per job.

Qualification. The difference between a calendar of estimates and a calendar of wasted drives. Filtering for service area, homeowner status, and real intent before a lead reaches you, or before it's booked as an appointment.

Reporting and a paid model that matches risk. Pay-per-lead, pay-per-call, pay-per-appointment, or a managed retainer. The model tells you who's carrying the risk, and you want as much of it on the provider as you can get.

The channels that actually produce roofing leads

Not all roofing leads are born equal. Where a lead comes from predicts how it closes.

Local Services Ads (Google Guaranteed) put you at the very top of search with a badge and a pay-per-lead model. High intent, strong for roofing, and Google screens you first. Often the best single channel for a local roofer.

Google Search Ads catch "roof repair near me" and "roof replacement cost", buyer intent, but clicks run $10-$35 and you pay whether or not they call.

SEO and Google Business Profile are the long game: rank for local roofing terms and your cost per lead drops over time toward nearly free. Slow to start, compounding once it lands. That's the heart of roofing marketing.

Aggregator marketplaces bundle and resell demand, usually shared. Volume's easy; quality and exclusivity are the question.

A real lead gen service runs one or more of these for you and hands over the result. A reseller just buys aggregator inventory and marks it up. Ask which you're dealing with.

What roofing lead generation costs

Pricing tracks the model, and the model tells you who's taking the risk.

Pay-per-lead means a flat fee per form fill or call, common, and fine if the leads are exclusive and you're not paying for junk. Pay-per-call (roughly $20-$50 per exclusive roofing call) bills only when the phone actually rings with a real prospect. Pay-per-appointment ($100-$150+ per booked, qualified roofing estimate) puts the most risk on the provider. Managed retainers are a flat monthly fee for running your ads and SEO, predictable, but you carry the outcome.

One sanity check cuts through all of it: cost per lead ÷ close rate ÷ average job value should land inside roughly 8-12% of revenue. A roofer closing 30% of exclusive leads at a $12,000 average can pay real money per lead and still clear that bar easily. See the full numbers in how much roofing leads cost.

How to spot a roofing lead gen service worth paying for

Four questions separate the real ones from the resellers.

Are the leads exclusive to me? If they're shared, you're back to the 5% close and the footrace. Get it in writing.

Do you control the traffic, or resell someone else's? Owning traffic means they can actually optimize quality. Reselling means they can't.

Do I get credited for junk leads? Wrong service, wrong zip, renters, spam, a real partner doesn't bill those.

Can I see call recordings and a dashboard? Transparency is non-negotiable. If you can't hear the calls and track the spend, you can't manage the channel. The best roofing lead generation companies clear all four without flinching.

When to outsource lead gen vs build it yourself

Build it in-house when you've got time and patience: rank your own site, run your own Google Business Profile, gather reviews, and your cost per lead drops toward nothing over a year. That's the cheapest pipeline there is. It just takes months and consistency.

Outsource when you need trucks full now and don't have the time or the ad expertise in-house. The trade is cost: you're paying a margin for speed and skill. Most roofers run both, outsource for immediate volume, build SEO underneath so they're not renting leads forever.

The wrong move is outsourcing forever and never building anything you own. Rented pipelines work until the day you stop paying. Use bought leads to fund the build, not replace it.

Red flags in a roofing lead gen contract

The pitch always sounds great. The contract is where you find out what you actually bought. Five things to read before you sign.

Shared leads dressed up as "qualified." If the agreement won't promise leads are sold once, to you only, assume they're shared. That single word decides whether you close 30% or 5%.

No junk-credit policy. Wrong service, wrong zip, renters, spam, telemarketers. Those should cost you nothing. A program that bills them and shrugs is padding its numbers with your money.

Long lock-ins and big minimums. A confident provider lets you start small and leave if it's not working. Twelve-month contracts and high monthly minimums protect them, not you. Month-to-month is a sign they trust their own leads.

No call recordings or dashboard. If you can't hear the calls and track spend against closed jobs, you can't manage the channel or prove ROI. Transparency isn't optional.

Fuzzy traffic source. "Our proprietary network" with no detail usually means resold aggregator inventory. Ask where the leads come from. Search and Local Services Ads are good answers; vagueness is a flag.

None of this means lead gen is a scam, done right it's one of the best growth levers a roofer has. It means the contract is where "done right" gets confirmed. Read it like the invoices depend on it, because they do. The best roofing lead generation companies clear all five without a fight.
