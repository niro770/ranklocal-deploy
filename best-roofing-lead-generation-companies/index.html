Best Roofing Lead Generation Companies (2026)

Most "best roofing lead company" lists rank whoever pays the affiliate commission. This one ranks on the only thing that decides whether you make money: are the leads exclusive, and do the unit economics work for a five-figure ticket. Below is the field roofers actually shop, what model each runs, and who it fits.

The single fault line across all of them is exclusive versus shared. Shared roofing leads close around 5% because you're racing four other roofers to the same homeowner. Exclusive leads close toward 30% because nobody else is on the phone. Keep that gap in mind as you read. It's worth more than any feature list.

How this list is scored

Every company gets judged on the same four things:

  • Model, pay per lead, pay per call, pay per appointment, or aggregator marketplace
  • Exclusive or shared, the number-one driver of your close rate
  • Pricing signal, what the public terms suggest you'll pay
  • Best for, the kind of roofing operation it fits

Model: Pay per lead · Exclusive: Yes (leads go to you) · Best for: essentially every local roofer.

How to choose, fast

Two questions settle it. Can your office answer in seconds and close on the phone? If yes, exclusive calls (RankLocal, ResultCalls) or LSAs win. If no, leads die in voicemail, buy booked appointments and stop bleeding the gap.

Then run one number for any provider: cost per lead ÷ close rate ÷ average job value. If it lands inside 8-12% of revenue, buy more. A roofer closing 30% of exclusive leads at a $12,000 ticket clears that easily; the same roofer on 5% shared leads rarely does.

And get exclusivity in writing, every time.

Questions to ask any roofing lead company before you pay

Forget the brand names for a minute. Whoever you're talking to, these five questions tell you most of what you need to know, and how they answer tells you the rest.

  1. "Are the leads exclusive to me, sold once?" If they hedge, they're shared. This is the whole ballgame.
  2. "Do you generate the traffic, or resell someone else's?" Owning the traffic means they can control quality. Reselling aggregator inventory means they can't.
  3. "What happens when I get a junk lead?" A real partner credits wrong-service, wrong-zip, renter, and spam leads. One that bills them is selling you noise.
  4. "Can I hear call recordings and see a dashboard?" No transparency, no way to manage the channel or prove ROI. Non-negotiable.
  5. "Is there a contract, or can I cancel?" Month-to-month means they're confident. Long lock-ins protect them, not you.

A company that answers all five cleanly and in writing is worth testing. One that dodges question one or three isn't worth your card number, no matter how slick the dashboard demo looks. Run a small test budget first, track cost per signed job, and scale only what proves out, the math framework is in how much roofing leads cost.

Frequently asked questions

What's the best roofing lead generation company? There's no single answer. It depends on whether you want exclusive calls, booked appointments, or marketplace volume, and how fast your office responds. For exclusive calls and inspections, providers like RankLocal and ResultCalls fit; for top-of-search, Local Services Ads; for sheer volume, the aggregators. Match the model to your operation.

Are roofing leads from Angi and Modernize exclusive? Generally no. Those marketplaces typically sell shared leads to several contractors, which is why close rates run lower. Always confirm, and price the shared model against exclusive before committing budget.

How much do roofing lead companies charge? Exclusive calls run about $20-$50 each, booked appointments $100-$150+, and shared marketplace leads vary. The number that matters is cost per signed job, see how much roofing leads cost.

Which is better, buying leads or Local Services Ads? Both can work, and many roofers run them together. LSAs give you exclusive, top-of-search leads you manage yourself; buying exclusive leads or appointments hands you qualified prospects with less management. Test both and judge by cost per won job.

Should I use more than one roofing lead company? Often, yes. Many roofers run Local Services Ads plus one exclusive-lead provider, then drop whichever delivers the worst cost per signed job. Test a small budget with each, track results, and concentrate spend on what proves out.
